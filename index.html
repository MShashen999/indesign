  1. 1 Pages Panel (Window > Pages) එකට ගොස් ඉහළින් ඇති A-Master එක මත double-click කරන්න.

  4. 4 පිටුවල "A" ලෙස අකුරක් වැටෙන අතර, එය පිටු වලට යද්දී අංක වලට auto මාරු වේ.

4 Full Booklet English Content

පහත තියෙන Text කොටස් ඔයාගේ InDesign document එකේ අදාල පිටු වලට copy කරගන්න.

Pages 04-05: Graphic Elements

GRAPHIC ELEMENTS Graphic elements are the building blocks of any design. 1. Line: The most basic element. Used to direct the eye or create boundaries. 2. Shape: Geometric (circles, squares) or organic (natural) forms. 3. Texture: The surface quality or feel of a design (visual or tactile). 4. Space: The area around, between, or within components (Positive vs Negative space). 5. Form: A three-dimensional object with volume and mass. 6. Color: Used to evoke emotion and define brand identity. 7. Value: The lightness or darkness of a color.

Pages 06-07: Graphic Principles

GRAPHIC PRINCIPLES Principles guide how elements are arranged to create effective communication. - Balance: The distribution of visual weight (Symmetrical vs Asymmetrical). - Contrast: Differences between elements to create emphasis (e.g., Black vs White). - Emphasis: The focal point of the design that draws attention. - Hierarchy: Organizing content to show order of importance. - Rhythm: Repeating elements to create a sense of movement. - Unity: When all elements belong together, creating a cohesive design.

Pages 08-09: Color Theory

COLOR THEORY Color theory is the art and science of using color. The Color Wheel: - Primary Colors: Red, Yellow, Blue. - Secondary Colors: Orange, Green, Purple (mixed from primary colors). - Tertiary Colors: Created by mixing primary and secondary colors. Color Schemes: - Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the wheel. - Analogous: Colors next to each other on the wheel. - Warm Colors: Reds, Oranges, Yellows. - Cool Colors: Blues, Greens, Purples.

Pages 10-11: Color Modes

COLOR MODES: RGB VS CMYK Understanding color modes is crucial for production quality. RGB (Red, Green, Blue): - Additive color model. - Used for digital screens (Web, Mobile, TV). - Colors are created by mixing light. CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black): - Subtractive color model. - Used for printing processes. - Colors are created by mixing physical inks.

Pages 12-13: Raster vs Vector

RASTER VS VECTOR Raster Images: - Made of Pixels (tiny dots). - Resolution-dependent. - Examples: Photographs (.JPG, .PNG). - Software: Adobe Photoshop. Vector Graphics: - Made of Paths (mathematical formulas). - Resolution-independent (Scalable to any size). - Examples: Logos, Illustrations (.AI, .EPS). - Software: Adobe Illustrator.

Pages 14-15: Typography

TYPOGRAPHY Typography is the arrangement of type to make text legible and appealing. - Serif Fonts: Small strokes at the end of letters (e.g., Times New Roman). - Sans-Serif Fonts: Fonts without strokes (e.g., Arial, Helvetica). - Kerning: Space between individual letters. - Leading: Space between lines of text. - Alignment: Left, Center, Right, or Justified.

5 TOC සහ අවසන් Export එක

Auto පටුන (TOC) හදන හැටි:

  • 1. සෑම මාතෘකාවකටම Paragraph Style එකක් ලබා දෙන්න.
  • 2. පටුන එන පිටුවට ගොස් Layout > Table of Contents යන්න.
  • 3. එහිදී එම Paragraph Style එක ඇතුළත් කර OK කරන්න. පටුන ඉබේම සැකසේ.

Export for Printing:

  • • File > Export ගොස් Format එක Adobe PDF (Print) දෙන්න.
  • • Adobe PDF Preset එකට [High Quality Print] තෝරන්න.
  • • Marks and Bleeds tab එකට ගොස් 'Crop Marks' සහ 'Use Document Bleed Settings' ටික් කරන්න.
